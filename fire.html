Precautions for Fire-Outbreak

Do's:

  • Dial the emergency number (e.g., 911) immediately to report the fire and request assistance.
  • Notify people in the vicinity of the fire to ensure their safety. Alert neighbors, family members, or coworkers if you're in a public place.
  • If the fire is in your vicinity and you can't control it, prioritize your safety and evacuate the area as quickly as possible.
  • If there is smoke, stay low to the ground where the air is less toxic. Crawl if necessary to avoid inhaling smoke.
  • Before opening doors, feel them with the back of your hand. If a door is hot, don't open it; it may lead to a fire-affected area..

Don'ts:

  • Do not delay evacuating to gather belongings or valuables. Your safety is the top priority.
  • Never use gasoline or other flammable liquids to try to control a fire. This can lead to explosions and greater danger.
  • Even if you hear alarms or see smoke, don't assume someone else has already called for help. Make the emergency call yourself.
  • Avoid jumping from high floors of a building. It's safer to wait for rescue personnel..
  • If you are trapped, don't hide. Signal for help by using a flashlight, waving a cloth, or making noise..
